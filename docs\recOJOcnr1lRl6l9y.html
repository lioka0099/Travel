Attractions

Fushimi Inari Shrine, located in Kyoto, is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that form a tunnel up the mountain. It's a breathtaking sight and a great spot for hiking. The shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Shinto god of rice.

Tokyo Skytree, located in Tokyo, is the tallest tower in the world and offers panoramic views of the city from its observation decks. It's a popular spot for tourists and locals alike. The tower is designed to withstand earthquakes and typhoons.

Mount Fuji, located on the main island of Honshu, is Japan's highest mountain and a sacred symbol of the country. It's a popular destination for hikers and photographers. The mountain is considered an active volcano, although it has not erupted since 1707.
