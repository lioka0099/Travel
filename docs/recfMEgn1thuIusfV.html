Top Attractions in Italy

The Colosseum, Rome: The Colosseum is an elliptical amphitheatre that is one of Rome's most iconic landmarks and a testament to the city's rich history. It is one of the largest amphitheaters in the Roman Empire, and could hold up to 50,000 spectators who came to watch gladiator battles, animal hunts, and public executions.

Grand Canal, Venice: The Grand Canal is the main waterway through Venice, lined with stunning examples of Venetian architecture. It is a popular tourist destination, with many visitors taking gondola rides or strolling along the canal's banks to take in the sights.

Leaning Tower of Pisa, Pisa: The Leaning Tower of Pisa is a freestanding bell tower known for its unintended tilt. The tilt is due to the soft ground it was built on, which caused the foundation to shift over time. Despite its tilt, the tower remains a beloved and iconic symbol of Italy.
