Must-Visit Attractions in Japan

Fushimi Inari Shrine, Kyoto - Known for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, Fushimi Inari Shrine is a breathtaking sight. A fun fact about this shrine is that it's dedicated to Inari, the Shinto god of rice and industry.

Meiji Shrine, Tokyo - Dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shoken, this shrine is a serene escape from the bustling city. Interestingly, the shrine is located in a tranquil forested area that was once a site for the Emperor's leisure activities.

Kiyomizu-dera Temple, Kyoto - A UNESCO World Heritage site, Kiyomizu-dera Temple is famous for its wooden stage protruding over the valley below, offering a panoramic view of Kyoto. The temple's name, which translates to "Pure Water Temple," is derived from the pure waters that run beneath it.
